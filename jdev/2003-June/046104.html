On Saturday 21 June 2003 13:08, Bart van Bragt wrote:
> I was adjusting my email spam filters, again, and that started me
> wondering about Jabber and spam.
>
> IMO there are not many provisions to prevent spam from being
> sent/received in the jabber protocol. The only thing you can do is:
> - block everything from anyone not in your roster except auth
> requests - blacklist specific servers (very temporary solution)
> - Filter on certain words/patterns (brrrrr, no a very clean
> solution)
>
> I don't know of any clients/servers that implement the first
> 'solution'. Has this been though about? Any idea how spam can be
> prevented or at least reduced?

I was wondering about this a few days ago also. With the central JUD 
at jabber.org, it is very easy to get valid jid for quite a few 
people ... And I think, as soon as jabber get a certain marketshare, 
spammers will discover this network. I already received a few 
'unwanted' messages.

I think, the first 'solution' would be a start, and with some limits 
on how many auth request per time... Still one could think of ways 
surpassing such limits.
