Neil Stevens wrote:
> On Saturday June 21, 2003 05:58, Sebastiaan Deckers wrote:
>>It is good to have basic systems already in place, which allow total
>>blocking of messages.  And a more elaborate filter system at the server
>>level is (relatively) trivial to implement.
Spam is one of the biggest problems with Email nowadays, everyone is
yelling that this can only be stopped with fundamental changes to SMTP
and not with more filtering or more legislation. So IMO we should take a
look at the proposed improvements to SMTP and see if we can implement
those in Jabber in advance.

> Your two paragraphs answer each other.  Once XMPP is popular, open 
> registration demo servers can and will have to be discontinued.
True, but one of the strong points of XMPP/Jabber is that it's an open
network. Everyone can create there own server and plug that into the
Jabber network, IMO it's fairly hard to discriminate between a
legitimate company/isp/whatever and a spammer :\ So spammers won't even
need public servers (although they are more convenient than setting up
your own spam server).
