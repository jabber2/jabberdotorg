Matthias Wimmer Wrote: 

> A JID has up to 1024 B not 1024 b ;)

Just to be picky (XMPP Core, section 3.1): 

	Each allowable portion of a JID (node identifier, domain 
	identifier, and resource identifier) MUST NOT be more than 
	1023 bytes in length, resulting in a maximum total size 
	(including the '@' and '/' separators) of 3071 bytes.

This means a JID has up to 3071B :)
