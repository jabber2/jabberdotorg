O'Reilly Open Source Convention
Call for Participation
July 26-30, 2004 in Portland, OR
http://conferences.oreilly.com/oscon/

OSCON is the only event where open source leaders and 
practitioners of every persuasion gather to integrate projects, 
launch revolutionary new ideas, push the boundaries of their 
respective technologies--and change the way we live and do 
business. 

You're invited to take a role in the next OSCON by becoming 
a speaker. (You'd be in good company--Robert Lefkowitz, 
George, Freeman, and Esther Dyson, Milton Ngan, and Tim 
O'Reilly are all confirmed speakers.) "Opening the Future: 
Discover, Develop, Deliver" is the theme for 2004, guiding 
tracks devoted to Linux, PHP, Python, Perl, MySQL and 
PostgreSQL, Apache, XML, Applications, Ruby, and Security. 
For more information on how to submit a proposal for a tutorial 
or session, sign up for a Lightning Talk, or suggest a panel, 
visit: http://conferences.oreillynet.com/cs/os2004/create/e_sess

Want to see some of last year's presentions? Go to: 
http://conferences.oreillynet.com/pub/w/23/presentations.html

There's also another way to help shape OSCON: Take a 
minute (literally) and fill out a short survey to help us make 
it an even better experience for you. And if that's not enough 
motivation, answering those few brief questions also enters 
you in a raffle to win a free pass to OSCON. Deadline to 
complete the survey is February 9.
http://conferences.oreillynet.com/survey/oscon04_planning

OSCON is the place where open source practitioners of every 
stripe come together to learn useful skills, discover what's 
new, "cross-fertilize" projects, and get fired up about the 
fact that their collaborative efforts are finally paying off. We 
hope you'll join us in Portland for the latest discoveries and 
developments along with the business, technical, political, 
and legal minds behind them.
