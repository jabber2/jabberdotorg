If you mean the O'Reilly book 'Programming Jabber' (which I highly 
recommend; though the oldest book, I found it indespensible for getting 
into Jabber), check out O'Reilly's site:

	http://www.oreilly.com/catalog/jabber/index.html

Available online via O'Reilly's Safari subscription service, this book 
can also be bought in dead-tree form from any of the major online book 
sellers, like

Amazon.com
http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/tg/detail/-/0596002025/qid=1075435012/sr=1-1/ref=sr_1_1/102-3302917-0119332?v=glance&s=books

Barnes & Noble
http://search.barnesandnoble.com/booksearch/isbnInquiry.asp?isbn=0596002025&itm=1

If, however, you mean one of the few other books that discuss Jabber in 
one aspect or another--'Jabber Developer's Handbook', 'Jabber 
Programming'--simply visit an online bookstore and do a search on 'Jabber'.

As for docs on the Jabber server source code, the source code IS the 
documentation at this point, both for jabberd v1.4.x and for the new, 
completely rewritten jabberd v2.  However, you may also want to keep 
your eyes on the Jabberd 2 Documentation Project, located at

	http://www.jabberdoc.org
