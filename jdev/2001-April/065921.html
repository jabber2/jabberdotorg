Currently, 
  I'm having a bit of problems getting my project up on Sourceforge,
but I have the beginnings of an XDB handler written in Java. My
company has recommended that we opensource it - so we all benefit. If
you're in the mood to tinker with it, feel free. Until the Sourceforge
account gets straightened out, just send me an email (matt at vedalabs.com)
and I'll be glad to send it to you. You should be able to make it talk
to any JDBC friendly database.

What works (that I know):
1) plaintext auth
2) 0k auth
3) rosters
4) temporary vCards

What doesn't work:
1) filters
2) digest authentication
3) offline message storage
4) jabber:iq:last handling
and probably countless other things that keep 
finding their way into the protocol 

Anyway - what I need are people testing, tinkering, and complaining.

It's in a very early state, and will probably go through a number
of major mutations as I get feedback, etc.

Take care to all.
