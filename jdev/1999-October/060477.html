At least in Michigan, I don't think it is possible to have a Not for
Profit S-Corp. Pitty though, I hear they're a lot easier to set up. As for
time, I'm not sure. But an S-Corp isn't to bad at all. Articles of
Incorporation are about it. Of course we'll need a board of directors and
such too. A good source of information on this might be distributed.net.
They did the same kind of thing -- a not for profit corporation started
with people from across the US. It seems to work.

Money will also be a concern. It costs money to become a corporation. Not
only State fees, but also lawyer costs and taxes. We should find some way
to get funding. Not sure how though.

Non profit takes even more jumps and leaps to get done (I'm possibly doing this too).  An average corporation startup will be just under $1000. Non-profit is definately the way to go, but again, it's a long process, and if it's started now, I would say just let it slowly happen, it'll take a bit of time.
