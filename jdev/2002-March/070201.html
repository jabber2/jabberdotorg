The problem here is that if you want to use features that aren't supported by your favorite Jabber server, your only real choice is to run your own.  Jabber is extremely decentralized, to the point where having your own Jabber server is almost as important as having your own SMTP server.  Your ISP provides an SMTP server, so if they won't provide you with a Jabber server, it's only reasonable for them to expect you to provide your own.  (IPv4 over IPv4 tunneling is an interesting way of getting around the issue, but I'm choosing to focus on a different method, HTTP proxying.)

 - Dave
