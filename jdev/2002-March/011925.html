You know that you can't do straight XMLRPC through jabber right? There is a namespace for it 
and everything. If you pick up DJ Adams' Programming Jabber, there is a section on doing 
JabberRPC, in java, python, and perl. Also, JabminRPC is a server component that does 
administration and it uses purely iq:rpc. I ended up using mister DJ's perl Jabber::Connection 
and Jabber::RPC libs (really the Jabber::RPC libs are just wrappers around an XMLRPC parser 
so its not hard to write or substitute your own XMLRPC parser). With all of that said, I dont quite 
think you understand the Jabber Protocol well enough. You can easily setup two clients and send 
whatever you want inside of the confines of the Jabber protocol. A more simplistic approach to 
RPC would be passing simple messages within the <message/>, more complex would be 
embedding XMLRPC inside of a <message/>, and the proper way to do it is to wrap the 
XMLRPC in an <iq xmlns='jabber:iq:rpc'/>. I think overall, you need to think simple :)
