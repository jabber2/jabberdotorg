Hello,

The solution turned out to be something silly: we updated our ODBC-driver
(since we noticed a new one was released) and then it suddenly seemed to
work....  except for offline storage of messages.  After trying a little it
turned out some things were not accepted by the database. I had to edit
"xdb_sql.xml" to fix that.  I changed 'date' (db2 doesn't like the timestamp
previously used)  and 'priority'. Don't know why, but db2 also didn't like
that one, so I disabled the priority. That did the trick, so now everything
is running for a while, and it works fine...
