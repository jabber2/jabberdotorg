> > Moreover, I noticed that my Jabber Server automatically lowercase the

> 
> Yes, US-ASCII characters in JIDs are "lowercased" (actually case-folded,
> in accordance with stringprep). 

All unicode characters are case-folded in JIDs, according to the
nodeprep profile. US-ASCII characters only were "lowercased" in the
legacy Jabber protocol. That is a minor incompatibility between XMPP and
legacy implementations, but IMHO that is a change in a good direction.
