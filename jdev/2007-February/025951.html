FOSTEL 2007

Paris, April 4 and 5.

Again this year there will be a Free/Open Source Telephony Summit (FOSTEL).
After three years in which the German Unix User Group (GUUG) organized this
event in Germany, we are moving to Paris this year, where the OpenWengo
project is taking the lead in organizing the event.

And we'll have some more changes: Voice and chat and other forms of real-time
cooperation over the Internet are moving closer together all the time. Thats
the reason we are widening our scope from Telephony and VoIP to all sorts
of real-time collaboration over the Internet. This includes instant messaging
as well as shared whiteboards or communal text editing and many many other
things.

There will be no public conference like in the last years. Instead we
concentrate on the most important part: Bringing the developers together
to show what they are doing, to learn what others are doing, and to work
together on improving their software.

Whats not going to change is the open and spontaneous atmosphere. There is
no limit on the number of participants, like in the last years. We hope to
see many of you in Paris!

More information is available from www.fostel.org.

If you have any questions, please contact Dave Neary <dave.neary at wengo.com>
or Jochen Topf <jochen.topf at guug.de>.
