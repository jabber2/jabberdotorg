The openH323.org folks are integrating speex into their offerings.  Cross
platform C++ software that is VERY well written (but does have a rather
large footprint).  And there are also version of the library that are
running (or very close to running) on WinCE, .NET, VxWorks.  There is also a
SIP implementation along with H323 in their "OPAL" contribution.

If it's voice you're looking to transpit, Speex [ http://speex.org/ ] is
probabbly a better choice. It's an alternate codec, tuned more for the
purpose of voice compression than music. Speech in particular is
fundamentally simpler then music, and can be compressed to a much
smaller size while maintiaining quality, provided you know it's speech
you're working with. :)
