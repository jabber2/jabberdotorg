>> secure XMPP servers C2S and S2S traffic:
> 
>> http://wiki.xmpp.org/web/Securing_XMPP
> 
> Thanks.
> 
> As you've seen from the news over the last 24 hours, things are
> even worse than we thought.
> 
> Among other things, forcing the use of SSL/TLS is not enough. We
> need to be careful about what ciphersuites we allow. Some of the
> older, weaker ciphersuites need to be disabled (e.g., RC4 / MD5).
> We need to start preferring ciphersuites that enable perfect

be interesting to see how widely those are supported in current XMPP
software.
