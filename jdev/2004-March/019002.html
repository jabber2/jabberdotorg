 From my looks at http://www.iptel.org/ser/doc/jabgw/xjab-manual.html it 
looks like this is meant for a SIP user to be able to access Jabber 
functionality, by acting as a client proxy gateway.  It seems somewhat 
complex, and the configuration hard-codes the XMPP service 
names/domains to send out the connection.  As in most client proxy 
gateways, passwords are stored in cleartext in the gateway, and special 
addressing is required to get through the gateway.
