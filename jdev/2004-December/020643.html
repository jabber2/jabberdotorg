> Realistically, though, they do have to agree anyway.  Can you normally 
> telephone someone without the other end picking up the phone?

Realistically, the voip client gets the call, rings and asks the user.

The proposed invitation solution would lead to:
* A sends an invitation to B, with uri ;
* B receives the invitation, says ok and calls the uri ;
* A's voip client receives the call and A has to accept it.

The patch I have allows:
* A publishes its voip uri ;
* B decides to call ;
* A's voip client receives the call and A has to accept it.

Which is saner.
